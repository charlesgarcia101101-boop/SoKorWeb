Seoul

Seoul, the capital of South Korea, is a metropolis of skyscrapers, tech, temples, and tradition.

Busan

Busan, known for beaches, mountains, and temples, is South Korea’s second-largest city.

Dadohaehaesang National Park

The largest national park in Korea, filled with coastal beauty and islands.

Incheon

Incheon is a major transportation hub with a rich cultural blend near Seoul.

Jeju

Jeju is a volcanic island with natural wonders and rich local culture.

Bibimbap

Rice bowl with vegetables, spicy gochujang, and egg/meat — rich, spicy, and crunchy.

Bulgogi

Thin marinated beef slices, grilled or stir-fried — savory and satisfying.

Kimchi

Fermented vegetables with spices — spicy, tangy, and iconic in Korean cuisine.

Samgyeopsal

Grilled pork belly served with sides and wraps — Korean BBQ favorite.

Tteokbokki

Rice cakes simmered in spicy sauce, often served with eggs and fish cakes.

Japchae

Glass noodles stir-fried with vegetables — slightly sweet and savory.
